Pluralistic Alignment and Cultural Disagreements in AI

Objective : This assignment explored the pluralistic alignment problem, which arises when AI systems must align with diverse and often conflicting human values.

Introduction

Summary of my explorations in algorithmic fairness, bias detection, and ethical considerations within machine learning systems, completed as part of the coursework for AI, Decision-Making, and Society at MIT CSAIL. These problem focus on applying theoretical fairness frameworks, developing practical evaluations, and implementing mitigation strategies to address societal and ethical challenges posed by AI.

Methodology

  • Analyzed a global opinion dataset containing survey responses from multiple countries.
  • Conducted variance analysis to identify controversial topics—questions that revealed significant disagreement across cultural and regional groups.
  • Applied t-SNE dimensionality reduction and K-means clustering to visualize and quantify patterns of alignment and disagreement.

Findings

  • The question regarding “moral acceptability of sex between unmarried adults” exhibited the highest variance, highlighting deep cultural and moral divides.
  • Countries such as Turkey and India reflected contrasting average response patterns, indicating regional differences in societal norms.

Global responses clustered around moderate stances, with fewer extreme positions.

Impact

This analysis demonstrates the challenges of designing AI systems that align with diverse ethical viewpoints. By identifying sources of disagreement, such evaluations provide a foundation for creating AI systems that are sensitive to cultural pluralism.
